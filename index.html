Alejandro Quintero

Luthier / Quintero Guitars

The construction of each of my guitars is the product of about 150 hours of wood crafting and undetermined hours of varnish padding to achieve a good French polish finish.

The materials used for each guitar are carefully selected for their acoustic attributes and beauty, and, maintained under a humidity controled environment for many years in my shop before they are used in the construction of an instrument.

Woods

The process begins with materials that come from all around the world:

  • Spruce from Germany and Yugoslavia,
  • Western Red Cedar and Engelmann Spruce from Canada,
  • Sitka spruce and Yellow Cedar from Alaska
  • Rosewood from Madagascar, India, Africa Central and South America,
  • Ebony from Africa
